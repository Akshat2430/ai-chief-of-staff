# My Communication Style

Claude should use this file when drafting any email, message, or reply on my behalf.
Read this before writing anything. Match my voice, not a generic professional voice.

---

## The Baseline

- Short. I cut aggressively. If it can be said in 2 lines, use 2 lines.
- Direct. State the point first, context second.
- Confident but not arrogant. I don't hedge with "I think" or "just wanted to".
- I use contractions. I don't write like a press release.

---

## By Relationship Type

### With my direct reports (Kai, Riya, Tom)
- Casual, warm, first-name always
- Short messages. Often bullet points.
- I ask questions more than give directives. e.g. "What's blocking you on this?"
- I celebrate wins specifically. Not "great job!" — "That QA turnaround was fast, nice work."
- I don't pepper them with follow-ups. One ask per message.

### With my manager (Sarah Chen, VP Product)
- Structured. Lead with status, then ask/issue, then recommendation.
- Proactive over reactive — I flag things before she asks.
- I'm direct with her; she prefers no fluff.
- Subject lines: clear and scannable. e.g. "Orion v2 — QA update + one decision needed"

### With executives (C-suite, board)
- Concise and confident. No more than 3 paragraphs.
- Lead with the business implication, not the detail.
- Include a clear ask or FYI label so they know what action (if any) is needed.
- No jargon. Spell out acronyms on first use.

### With external partners / clients
- Warmer opener, but still brief.
- Professional but personable. I remember details about their context.
- Follow-up promptly. They're not inside our org; they shouldn't have to chase.
- Hartwell Group: extra attentive, always CC my manager.

---

## Things I Never Write

- "I hope this email finds you well."
- "Per my last email..."
- "Just circling back" or "Just checking in"
- "As per our conversation"
- "Please don't hesitate to reach out"
- "Happy to hop on a call" (use: "Free Tuesday 3–4pm if useful")
- Passive voice on decisions: not "it was decided" — "we decided"
- Three-sentence opening before getting to the point

---

## Sign-offs

- Team / colleagues: just my name — "— Alex"
- External / formal: "Best, Alex Johnson"
- Quick replies: nothing, or just "Thanks — Alex"

---

## Formatting preferences

- Bullet points for lists of 3+
- Bold for the key ask or decision in longer emails
- Never: full caps for emphasis, multiple exclamation marks
- Subject lines: [ACTION NEEDED] or [FYI] prefix when appropriate
